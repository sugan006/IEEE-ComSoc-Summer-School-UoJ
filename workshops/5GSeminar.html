Workshop Session: 5G: Foundations, Innovations, and Open Ecosystems

This seminar provides an exploration of 5G technology, its transformative capabilities, and the rapidly evolving ecosystem surrounding it. Participants will gain both theoretical knowledge and practical insights into 5G deployment, innovation, and open collaboration, equipping them to actively engage with and contribute to the next generation of mobile communications.

The seminar covers: Fundamentals of 5G: Core concepts, enabling technologies, network components, and a comparative look at improvements over previous generations; Capabilities & Envisaged Applications and Market Trends: Enhanced mobile broadband, ultra-reliable low-latency communications, massive IoT connectivity, and their potential to support revolutionizing applications, analysis of current 5G market dynamics across various regions; Open RAN: Introduction to the Open RAN (Radio Access Network) concept, key advantages and technical underpinnings that support a more open and innovative mobile infrastructure; Open Source 5G Frameworks: Review of leading open source 5G platforms, their architecture, contribution models, and roles in academic and industry research. Demonstrations: Demonstration of the team’s open source 5G testbed, showcasing integration of multiple platforms, real-world testing scenarios and opportunities for collaborative experimentation.

Prof Dileeka Dias

Dileeka Dias is a Professor in the Department of Electronic & Telecommunication Eng., University of Moratuwa, and a telecommunications expert with nearly four decades of experience in teaching, research, innovation, and industry collaboration. She holds a Ph.D. and M.Sc. from the University of California, Davis, and a B.Sc. from the University of Moratuwa. Her main interests are in wireless networks, focusing on low latency applictions, sensing and positioning, IoT, intelligent transport systems and disaster communications.

Rajaretnam Sairisan

Rajaretnam Sairisan recently competed his final year in the Electronic & Telecommunication Engineering degree program at the University of Moratuwa. He has experience in open-source 4G/5G development, game development, and research. He is the founder of Stealth Studio.

Methsara Dissanayeka

Methsara Dissanayeka holds a Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering and currently works as a Research Engineer at the Dialog–UoM Research Lab. He specializes in 5G core networks, embedded systems, with hands-on experience using USRP SDRs and the Open source 5G platforms. Methsara is passionate about turning advanced research into practical solutions and brings valuable insights from both academic and industry-driven projects.
